Abstract
The idea of the project is to use the ARMA (Auto-Regressive Moving Average) model, to enable a snakelike robot to predict its motion on the basis of the sensory data it collects. An ARMA model is a plausible option as it is efficient at analysing periodic data, which is a prominent charecterestic of serpentine gait. The predicitve behaviour of this model also depends on past collected data rather just utilising present data.
1. Introduction
A snake-like robot, due to its redundantly many degrees of freedom, has great mobility and flexibility allowing it to navigate though dense obstacle maps where conventional robots cannot traverse. A further, prviously unused advantage is the fact that its oscillatory movement allows the bot to use proximity sensors to map a wide portion of its environment.
1.1 Introduction to Problem
However, simply mapping its environment won't suffice for any practical purposes. The sensor data collected from the proximity sensors will be quite unintelligible to humans and it is not practical to feed this data directly to a serpenoid gait controller for autonomous navigation. We need to add an intermediate functional black-box that would implement digital signal processing techniques to predict the subsequent heading directions of the robot.
